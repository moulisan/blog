Knolling

Knolling is the practice of organizing objects in parallel or at 90Â° angles. The term has been popularized by artist Tom Sachs ; he picked it up from Andrew Kromelow when both were working at Frank Gehry's furniture fabrication shop. Gehry was designing chairs for furniture company Knoll , and Kromelow would arrange unused tools in a manner similar to Knoll furniture. Hence, knolling.
